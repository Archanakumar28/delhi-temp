About the Project

Ephemeral Delhi documents temporary architecture as a vital part of urban cultural life. These structures exist at the intersection of ritual, economy, and politics.

Rather than seeing them as temporary utilities, this project interprets them as designed environments rich in meaning. A pandal becomes a narrative space, a wedding tent an expression of aspiration, and a protest stage a claim to visibility.

What We Study

  • Religious Structures – devotion and spectacle
  • Social Spaces – identity and class
  • Political Structures – power and resistance
  • Commercial Spaces – urban rhythms
