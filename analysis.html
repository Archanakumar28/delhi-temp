Spatial & Cultural Analysis

This section moves beyond documentation to interpretation. By mapping and categorizing temporary structures across Delhi, the project identifies patterns in how space is used, transformed, and experienced.

The data reveals that temporary architecture is not randomly distributed—it reflects cultural concentration, political activity, and economic rhythms. Religious structures tend to cluster within residential neighborhoods, while protest architecture appears closer to administrative zones. Social structures such as wedding tents vary significantly in scale and design depending on locality and class context.

Through this analysis, the project demonstrates that temporary architecture is a key mechanism through which urban space is continuously produced and negotiated.
