Spatial & Cultural Analysis

Understanding Temporary Architecture

Temporary architecture in Delhi operates as a dynamic cultural system rather than a static built form. These structures emerge in response to specific events, transforming space through design, scale, and collective participation. Their significance lies not in permanence, but in their ability to reshape urban experience within a limited time frame.

Production of Space

Drawing from spatial theory, these structures demonstrate that urban space is continuously produced rather than fixed. A street becomes a ritual path, an open ground becomes a sacred enclosure, and a public site transforms into a space of resistance.

Space in Delhi is not stable — it is repeatedly constructed through cultural practice.

Performance and Temporality

These environments function as performative spaces where meaning is enacted through movement, gathering, and visual expression. Their temporality intensifies their impact, creating moments of heightened experience that dissolve once the structure is removed.

Memory and Disappearance

Despite their disappearance, these structures persist in memory. They leave behind traces in photographs, spatial recollections, and collective experience, challenging the idea that cultural significance depends on permanence.
