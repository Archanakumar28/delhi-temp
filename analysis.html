Analysis

Interpreting Temporary Architecture as Cultural Practice

Reading the Temporary City

Temporary architecture in Delhi offers a unique lens through which to understand the city. Unlike permanent structures, these spaces exist within a limited timeframe, yet their impact on spatial experience is immediate and profound. They reorganize movement, reshape visibility, and create temporary centers of attention within the urban landscape.

By examining these structures, the project shifts focus from static architecture to dynamic spatial processes. The city emerges not as a fixed entity, but as something continuously produced through cultural activity, ritual, and performance.

Architecture as Performance

Temporary structures function as performative spaces. Their meaning lies not only in their design, but in their use—how people gather, move, and interact within them. A festival pandal becomes meaningful through ritual participation, a protest stage through collective presence, and a wedding tent through social display.

These spaces are therefore not passive environments. They actively produce experience, transforming ordinary locations into sites of significance for a limited duration.

Production of Space

The project draws on the idea that space is socially produced rather than fixed. Temporary architecture demonstrates this clearly: an empty ground can become sacred, political, or celebratory depending on how it is constructed and used.

These transformations reveal that urban space is constantly negotiated. Authority, access, and visibility are redefined through the presence of these structures, even if only temporarily.

Key Cultural Insights

Impermanence

These structures reflect cultural ideas of transience, where meaning is not tied to permanence but to experience and duration.

Collective Memory

Even after dismantling, these spaces persist in memory, shaping how communities remember events and experiences.

Power & Visibility

Political and religious structures assert presence in public space, making certain identities and voices visible.

Urban Rhythms

The city follows cycles of construction and disappearance, revealing patterns tied to festivals, seasons, and social events.

Memory and Disappearance

One of the central questions of this project concerns what remains after these structures vanish. While their material form disappears, their impact persists in memory, documentation, and collective experience.

This highlights the importance of digital archiving. By recording these spaces, the project preserves moments that would otherwise be lost, allowing them to be studied, revisited, and interpreted beyond their physical lifespan.

Central Argument

Temporary architecture in Delhi functions as a performative cultural system that reshapes public space, produces collective memory, and reflects shifting social, religious, and political identities.
