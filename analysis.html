Spatial & Cultural Analysis

Understanding Temporary Architecture

Temporary architecture in Delhi operates as a dynamic cultural system rather than a fixed built form. These structures emerge in response to specific events, transforming space through design, scale, and collective participation. Their significance lies not in permanence, but in their ability to reshape urban experience within a limited time frame.

Production of Space

These structures demonstrate that space is continuously produced through social and cultural activity. Streets become ritual pathways, open grounds transform into sacred environments, and public sites evolve into spaces of resistance.

Urban space in Delhi is not static—it is repeatedly constructed through cultural practice.

Performance and Temporality

Temporary structures function as performative environments where meaning is created through participation, movement, and visual design. Their short lifespan intensifies their cultural impact, producing moments of heightened spatial experience.

Memory and Disappearance

Even after they are dismantled, these structures persist in memory. They leave behind traces in visual documentation and collective experience, suggesting that cultural meaning can exist beyond physical permanence.
