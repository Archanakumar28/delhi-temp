Tracing the Temporary

A Digital Humanities Archive of Delhi’s Ephemeral Architecture

Delhi is not only a city of permanence—it is a city of moments. Beyond its monuments and enduring structures exists a parallel landscape that appears, transforms space, and disappears.

Across neighborhoods, temporary architectures emerge—festival pandals, wedding tents, protest stages, and seasonal markets. They reorganize movement and reimagine public space.

These structures activate the city, turning ordinary spaces into sites of ritual, celebration, and expression.

These structures do not merely occupy space—they perform it.

Built to Transform

Temporary architecture reshapes urban space by turning everyday locations into sites of gathering, ritual, and spectacle. It reveals that space is continuously produced through use and collective presence.

Designed to Communicate

Through color, material, and form, these structures express identity, belief, and power. They function as visual languages embedded in cultural contexts.

Meant to Disappear

Built with impermanence in mind, these structures leave behind memory rather than material, challenging traditional ideas of heritage and permanence.

“The present contains nothing more than the past, and what is found in the effect was already in the cause.”

— Henri Bergson
