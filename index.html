Tracing the Temporary

A Digital Humanities Archive of Delhi’s Ephemeral Architecture

Delhi is not only a city of permanence—it is a city of moments. Beyond its monuments exists a parallel landscape that appears, transforms, and disappears.

Temporary architectures—pandals, tents, protest stages—reshape movement and gather communities, creating intense but short-lived spatial experiences.

These structures activate the city, turning ordinary spaces into sites of ritual and expression.

These structures do not merely occupy space—they perform it.

Built to Transform

Temporary architecture reshapes urban space, turning ordinary locations into sites of gathering, ritual, and spectacle.

Designed to Communicate

Through color, material, and form, these structures express identity, belief, and power.

Meant to Disappear

Built with impermanence in mind, these structures leave behind memory rather than material.
